Our safety philosophy

Our goal is to ensure that all students and project stakeholders are aware of the dangers associated with the Robotics First project. The safety rules and procedures must be applied by all participants without exception.

Julie Casgrain, our school technician is a certified first aid rescuer and our first contact in case of emergency.

Priority

SAFETY is a PRIORITY

Chemical Products

  • All chemicals (glue, paint, batteries or others) are maintainted in a register of Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS) and must be stored immediately after use
  • Extinguish a fire with baking soda
  • Use a safety container for spills
  • Clean your work space after usage

Using Batteries

  • Power-off the robot when working on it
  • Ensure no stored energy is present (electric-pneumatic or other)
  • Ensure wires are not left unexposed
  • Beware of short circuits
  • Report unsafe conditions to coaches and mentors

Protection Gear

  • Safety glasses
  • Gloves
  • Closed shoes
  • Ear protection
  • Hair tie
  • Avoid wearing any jewelry

Safe Environment

  • Beware of bulky structures, sprinklers or any other obstacle
  • Robot should be lifted by 2-4 people
  • Use legs to lift and protect your lower back by bending knees
  • Robot should be transported on a cart to avoid injuries and/or damage
  • Fire extinguishers are always carried in the cart, near the robot
